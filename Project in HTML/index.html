When did Coffee first appear in the Phillipines

Coffee was first introduced to the Philippines during the late 17th century. It is believed that coffee seeds were brought to the archipelago by a Spanish Franciscan friar named Pedro de Alfaro, who planted the first coffee tree in the town of Lipa, Batangas. The favorable climate and fertile soil of the Philippines proved to be ideal for coffee cultivation, and the crop quickly thrived. Coffee production grew steadily, and by the 19th century, the Philippines became one of the largest coffee exporters in the world. The coffee industry played a significant role in the country's economy, shaping local communities and contributing to its cultural heritage. However, the industry faced challenges in the 20th century due to various factors such as plant diseases and the introduction of more profitable crops, leading to a decline in coffee production. Nevertheless, coffee remains an integral part of Philippine culture, and the country continues to produce and enjoy a diverse range of local coffee varieties. 1601. (Source: Magazine)
